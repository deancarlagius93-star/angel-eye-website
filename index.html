Origin Story

How Angel Eye Was Born

Angel Eye came from a real marine maintenance problem. An engineer was testing an anchor chain counter while working on wiring. He needed one person inside the cockpit watching the screen while he worked elsewhere.

That second person was not repairing anything — they were simply acting as a second pair of eyes.

Angel Eye solves that. Place the camera by the display, stream it to your phone, and work alone.

Real-world uses

Made for technicians. Useful for everyone.

Marine Engineers & Skippers

  • Watch chain counters while repairing windlass wiring.
  • Monitor bilges, shaft seals, belts and generators.
  • Check exhaust water flow during generator startup.
  • Observe hydraulic gangways, pumps and engine-room components.

Mechanics

  • Check brake lights, reverse lights and indicators alone.
  • Look under engines while they are running.
  • Monitor fluid leaks, suspension movement and exhaust areas.
  • Record diagnostic videos for customers.

Technicians & Electricians

  • Watch control panels while adjusting wiring elsewhere.
  • Monitor equipment energising after breaker tests.
  • Inspect machinery safely from a distance.
  • Check pumps, motors, conveyors and inaccessible areas.

Home & Everyday Use

  • Plug it in at home to monitor pets while at work.
  • Use it as a temporary security camera.
  • Watch deliveries, garages, sheds or holiday homes.
  • Move it around without permanent installation.
